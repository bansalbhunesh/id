## Design Context

### Users
IDBI Innovate judges, bank executives, credit underwriters, and product/technology reviewers evaluating a Stage-1 PoC for MSME financial health scoring. They need to see a credible underwriting decision quickly, inspect why the decision is defensible, and understand how the prototype maps to bank governance and Stage-2 sandbox integration.

### Brand Personality
Sober, decisive, regulator-ready. The interface should feel like a bank-grade credit cockpit rather than a startup landing page: dense enough for repeated review, clear enough for a live three-minute demo, and confident without hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
Elegant private-banking review desk: light editorial surfaces, a persistent case rail, ledger-like evidence rows, restrained IDBI-adjacent green, muted amber/risk accents, and deliberate shadcn-inspired primitives translated into static HTML. Avoid generic fintech gradients, oversized marketing heroes, glass effects, and card dumps.

### Design Principles
- Lead with the rejected-to-approved NTC decision reversal and make the evidence inspectable in the first viewport.
- Show governance as product surface, not appendix: explainability, audit, model limits, and fairness controls must be visible.
- Prefer compact banker workflows over marketing copy; every visible element should support a judge demo or credit review.
- Use a sidebar-plus-dossier shell, command/status header, segmented tabs, quiet badges, and tabular proof panels before adding any new decorative treatment.
- Keep the app deployable as a static frontend served by FastAPI; no build step or fragile external asset dependency.

02 Brief About The Idea

Consented alternate data, turned into a decision a bank can defend.

01
What it is.

An explainable MSME Financial Health Card that scores businesses from consented alternate data.

02
Who it's for.

New-to-Credit (NTC) and New-to-Bank (NTB) MSMEs that traditional bureau-based underwriting rejects outright.

03
Inputs.

GST filings, UPI transaction trails, Account Aggregator bank statements, EPFO records.

04
Outputs.

A 0–100 score, an A–E grade, an eligible credit limit, and a plain-language improvement plan.

UdyamPulse · IDBI Innovate 2026
03 Opportunities

Explainability and auditability, by default.

Differentiation

Beyond a number

Most alternate-data scoring tools return a score. UdyamPulse returns a decision a bank can defend to a regulator and a borrower can act on.

Approach

Two engines, one verdict

Independent, cross-checkable scoring methods run side by side and show explicitly what bureau-only underwriting would have decided vs. alternate data.

USP

The regulatory gap

Of 127 AI-using regulated entities surveyed, only 15 use SHAP/LIME explainability and only 18 keep audit logs (RBI). UdyamPulse ships both by default.

07 Architecture

One service. Two engines. A full audit trail.

Single FastAPI Service

One process serves the API and the frontend — one Dockerfile, one deploy target.

Rule + ML Dual Engine

Transparent 5-pillar rules alongside a trained model with exact Shapley attribution.

Temporal Evidence Gate

Dated outcomes, 365-day maturity, chronological development/calibration/OOT, segment-volume controls.

Promotion & Audit Gate

Pseudonymous SHA-256 audit plus fail-closed pilot startup until model, OOT, identity, and storage pass.
